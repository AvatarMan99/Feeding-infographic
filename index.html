Infant Development & Feeding Skills

Birth - 4 Months

Reflexes dominate: Rooting reflex (turns head when cheek is touched), sucking reflex, and tongue thrust reflex present. Head and neck control beginning to develop. Coordinates sucking, swallowing, and breathing patterns.
üçº Appropriate Foods
Liquid foods only: Breast milk or iron-fortified infant formula. No solid foods needed or recommended during this period.
4 - 6 Months
üéØ Developmental Skills
Transitioning: Tongue thrust reflex begins to fade. Good head and neck control achieved. Can sit with support. Shows interest in food by leaning forward and opening mouth. Beginning to move food from front to back of mouth.
üçº Appropriate Foods
Introduction to solids: Continue breast milk or formula. Begin iron-fortified infant cereal (rice, oatmeal, barley) mixed with breast milk or formula to thin consistency. Can introduce smooth, pureed single-ingredient foods.
6 - 8 Months
üéØ Developmental Skills
Expanding abilities: Sits without support. Uses up-and-down munching motion. Begins moving tongue from side to side. Can transfer objects from hand to hand. Starts bringing food to mouth with hands (palmer grasp).
ü•Ñ Appropriate Foods
Expanding variety: Breast milk or formula continues. Iron-fortified infant cereals. Pureed or mashed vegetables (sweet potato, carrots, peas, green beans). Pureed or mashed fruits (banana, avocado, applesauce, pears). Pureed meats or beans. Introduce one new food at a time (3-5 day rule).
8 - 10 Months
üéØ Developmental Skills
Self-feeding emerges: Develops pincer grasp (thumb and forefinger). Begins to self-feed with fingers. Can drink from a cup with help. Chewing with rotary jaw movements begins. Shows food preferences and may refuse foods.
ü•ò Appropriate Foods
Soft table foods: Continue breast milk or formula. All previous foods plus soft, cooked vegetables in small pieces. Soft fruits cut into pieces. Ground or finely chopped meats. Cheese cubes or grated cheese. Whole grain bread or crackers. Small pasta shapes. Avoid choking hazards.
10 - 12 Months
üéØ Developmental Skills
Increasing independence: Refined pincer grasp. Attempts to use spoon (messily). Drinks from sippy cup independently. Chews with rotary jaw movements. Participates actively in feeding. May show strong food preferences.

Most family foods: Breast milk or formula (can transition to whole milk at 12 months). Chopped table foods with family. Variety of fruits and vegetables. Proteins: eggs, meat, poultry, fish, beans. Whole grain breads, cereals, pasta. Yogurt and cheese. Three meals plus snacks. Continue avoiding honey, choking hazards (whole grapes, nuts, popcorn), and excessive juice.
Important Notes: Always introduce new foods one at a time to watch for allergic reactions. Avoid honey before age 1. Cut foods into small pieces to prevent choking. Consult healthcare provider for individual guidance.
